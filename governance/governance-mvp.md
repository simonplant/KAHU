# KAHU Governance MVP — Pre-Community Phase

**Version 1.0** — March 2026  
**Stage**: Early development (10-50 users)

This document outlines how KAHU decisions get made when the community is small, and how governance structures will evolve as it grows.

---

## Context: Why Governance Matters Early

KAHU is built on values: care over extraction, community ownership over corporate control, consciousness development over profit.

These values must be embedded in *how we make decisions* from day one. A governance process that's added later, as an afterthought, will reflect the values already baked in.

Getting it right early means:
- Users and contributors have voice from the start
- Cultural advisors are integrated into development
- No one person becomes an invisible dictator
- Decisions are transparent and reversible

---

## Phase 1: Core Team Governance (Months 1-6)

### 1.1 Decision-Making Structure

**Core Team** (5-10 people):
- Technical leads (guardian AI development)
- Community stewards (user relationships, feedback)
- Cultural advisors (1-2 representing different traditions)
- User representatives (early users with voice)

**No CEO**. No board. Just a team.

### 1.2 Decision Types and Processes

**Tactical Decisions** (day-to-day implementation):
- Who decides? Technical lead for their domain
- Process: Announce to team, 24-hour input window, proceed unless major objection
- Example: Which emotional patterns to detect in patterns.py?

**Strategic Decisions** (direction, architecture, values):
- Who decides? Consensus of core team (all must agree or actively decline to block)
- Process: Proposal → discussion (3-5 days) → decision
- Example: Do we fine-tune on Llama 4 or stay with Claude API?

**Values/Ethics Decisions** (touches core mission):
- Who decides? Must include cultural advisors and at least one user rep
- Process: Proposal → deep discussion → consensus with escalation if needed
- Example: How do we handle cultural adaptation? What's appropriation vs. respect?

**User-Affecting Decisions** (changes users experience):
- Who decides? Must include user rep and cultural advisors
- Process: Propose to users first (feedback window), then team decides
- Example: Should guardians proactively reach out? How often?

### 1.3 Disagreement Protocol

**If Core Team Disagrees**:
1. **Name the disagreement** clearly (what are we actually disagreeing about?)
2. **Understand the perspectives** (why does each person hold their view?)
3. **Look for the middle path** (what satisfies the core concerns on all sides?)
4. **Time-box the discussion** (if not resolved in 3 discussion sessions, escalate)
5. **Escalate to cultural advisors** if values are in question
6. **Reversible decision** (agree to try one path, review after 2 weeks)

**No one person can block a decision indefinitely.**

### 1.4 Transparency

**What's Public**:
- Major decisions and rationale (published in DECISIONS.md)
- Strategic direction (shared quarterly)
- User feedback (aggregated, anonymized)
- Architecture choices (documented in technical docs)

**What's Private**:
- Individual user data (sacred)
- Personal information about team members
- Sensitive cultural information (with advisors' permission to keep private)
- Security vulnerabilities (until patched)

**Default**: Public unless there's a good reason otherwise.

## Phase 2: Community Council (Months 6-18, 50-500 users)

As the community grows, decision-making must scale.

### 2.1 The Community Council

**Composition**:
- 2-3 technical leads (guardian development, infrastructure)
- 3-5 community stewards (user voice, relationship health)
- 3-5 cultural advisors (representing different traditions)
- 5-10 user representatives (elected or appointed)

**Total: ~15-20 people**

### 2.2 Council Decision Process

**Quarterly Strategic Planning**:
- Council meets (in person if possible, video if not)
- Reviews: user feedback, technical progress, emerging issues
- Sets direction for next quarter
- Creates explicit goals and success measures

**Monthly Decisions**:
- Async voting on major proposals
- Discussion via forum/video for controversial items
- Consensus preferred, majority vote if needed
- All discussions logged and public (unless privacy reason)

**Rapid Decisions**:
- Tactical items handled by domain leads
- No vote needed if uncontroversial
- 24-hour async notification to council

### 2.3 User Representatives

**How They're Selected**:
- **Early phase** (20 users): All engaged users invited to council
- **Growth phase** (100+ users): 5-10 elected by community
- **Elections**: Annual, ranked-choice voting
- **Diversity**: Active effort to ensure representation across:
  - Cultural backgrounds
  - Life stages
  - Use cases
  - Geographic regions

**What They Do**:
- Bring user voice into decisions
- Provide feedback on proposed changes
- Help test new features
- Escalate user concerns
- Are NOT representatives of all users (they're one voice, not ambassadors)

### 2.4 Cultural Advisory Board

**Purpose**: Ensure KAHU respects and serves diverse traditions

**Composition** (3-5 advisors):
- Person with deep knowledge of one wisdom tradition
- Person with experience in cross-cultural work
- Person who represents often-overlooked context (Indigenous, non-Western, etc.)
- Diversity of expertise: theological, practical, academic

**What They Review**:
- Any guardian guidance that touches spirituality/ethics
- How cultural adaptation is working in practice
- Training data for appropriation or harm
- Community conflicts with cultural dimensions

**Their Authority**:
- Can veto decisions that represent cultural appropriation
- Can require changes to respect traditions
- Can recommend training or learning for team
- Can recommend bringing in additional advisors

**What They're NOT**:
- Spokespersons for their tradition (they're individuals)
- Responsible for all cultural sensitivity (team job)
- Required to sign off on everything

### 2.5 Decision Escalation

**Disagreement at Council Level**:
1. **Identify the core question** (what are we actually deciding?)
2. **Gather data** (what do users/research/advisors say?)
3. **Present options** (what are the real choices with tradeoffs?)
4. **Vote** if consensus impossible
   - Simple majority for routine decisions
   - 2/3 majority for values/ethics decisions
   - Unanimous if affects cultural sacred ground
5. **Implement** with clear deadline to review

## Phase 3: Distributed Governance (Year 2+, 500+ users)

Eventually, KAHU will be big enough that centralized governance is impossible and undesirable.

### 3.1 Core Principles for Distributed Governance

**Subsidiarity**: Decisions made at the lowest level that affects them
- Local community guardians: handled by local facilitators
- Protocol questions: handled by technical working groups
- Value/ethics questions: handled by larger councils

**Federated Structure**: Autonomous units (regions, cultures, practice areas) with alignment on core values

**Transparent Decision-Making**: All decisions documented, rationale public

**Reversibility**: Major decisions implemented as experiments, reviewed after period

### 3.2 Possible Future Structure

**Local Circles** (50-100 users in a region/tradition):
- Local facilitator (elected or appointed)
- Local cultural advisor
- Monthly reflection and feedback
- Decisions affecting only that circle made locally

**Working Groups** (cross-cutting technical/practice areas):
- Guardian Memory Working Group
- Emotional Intelligence Working Group
- Cultural Adaptation Working Group
- Protective Response Working Group
- Infrastructure Working Group

Each working group:
- 5-10 people (mix of experts and interested contributors)
- Monthly public meetings
- Decisions via consensus (or escalate if blocked)
- Reports quarterly to larger council

**Representative Council**:
- Reps from each local circle (1-2 per 100 users)
- Representatives from each working group
- Cultural advisory seats
- Makes quarterly strategic decisions
- Reviews/approves major changes

**Emergence**: What forms naturally from smaller units becomes the formal structure

### 3.3 How Values Stay Aligned as System Grows

**Values Constitution**:
- Core principles written explicitly (see vision/manifesto.md)
- Used as decision-making framework at all levels
- Reviewed annually with community input
- Can only be changed by supermajority (2/3+) vote

**Distributed Enforcement**:
- Each local circle holds others accountable to values
- Working groups document decisions against constitution
- If someone/some region drifts from values, other regions can stop collaboration
- Reputation matters (communities remember betrayals)

**Forks**: If KAHU ever becomes corrupted, communities can fork and continue independently

## Voting and Consensus Mechanisms

### 4.1 When to Use Consensus vs. Voting

**Aim for Consensus**:
- Values decisions
- Proposals affecting many groups
- When time allows (3-7 days discussion)
- Default mode

**Use Voting When**:
- Consensus stalled after good-faith discussion
- Time-sensitive decision needed
- Multiple valid options, one must be chosen
- Previous agreement to vote on this type of decision

### 4.2 Voting Processes

**Simple Majority** (Tactical decisions):
- >50% votes for
- Example: Which bug fix to prioritize?

**2/3 Majority** (Values/Ethics decisions):
- >67% votes for
- Example: Changes to cultural adaptation policy

**Supermajority** (Core values):
- >80% votes for
- Example: Changing the fundamental guardian specification

**Preferential Voting** (Multiple candidates):
- Ranked-choice voting
- First choice counts first, if tie, second choices counted, etc.
- Example: Electing community representatives

### 4.3 How Blocking Works (When Someone Has Deep Disagreement)

**In Consensus Mode**:
- One person can block a decision if they have genuinely principled objection
- Blocker must offer alternative path
- Discussion continues until consensus or escalation

**Escalation After Block**:
- If block stands after 3 discussion rounds, escalate to:
  - Cultural advisors (if ethics/values)
  - Technical leads (if technical)
  - Larger council (if strategic)
- Escalation group hears blocker and proposer
- Can override block if compelling reason, but this is serious

**No Arbitrary Blocking**: Block only if you'd rather see decision reversed than moved forward

## Feature Prioritization Process

### 5.1 Quarterly Planning

**Input Collection**:
- User feedback (what do they need?)
- Technical opportunities (what becomes possible?)
- Value alignment (what serves mission?)
- Resource reality (what can we actually do?)

**Prioritization Criteria** (in order):
1. **Does it serve user flourishing?** (core mission test)
2. **Is it culturally respectful?** (advisory input)
3. **Technical feasibility** (can we do it?)
4. **Impact scale** (how many users affected?)
5. **Effort required** (what's the resource cost?)

**Voting**:
- Each council member ranks top 10 priorities
- Votes weighted: community stewards 2x (they hear users), cultural advisors 2x (they catch harm)
- Top 3-5 priorities become next quarter focus

### 5.2 Transparency in Prioritization

**Published**:
- Why each feature was chosen or not chosen
- Trade-offs between competing priorities
- Resource constraints being faced
- Timeline for each priority

**Feedback Loop**:
- Users can request reconsideration
- If significant disagreement, go to council vote
- Quarterly review allows course correction

## Conflict Resolution

### 6.1 User-User Conflicts

**Not the Guardian's Role**: Guardians don't referee personal conflicts. 

**Possible Scenarios**:
- Two users are both seeing the same guardian (unlikely by design, but possible)
- Communities overlap and disagree on direction
- User disputes how their data was handled

**Process**:
1. **Clarity**: What exactly is the conflict?
2. **Facilitation**: Neutral third party (community steward) facilitates discussion
3. **Options**: Generate possible solutions together
4. **Agreement**: Try to reach agreement; if impossible, separate cleanly
5. **Escalate** only if something affects others

### 6.2 Team Conflicts

**If Core Team Disagrees** (see section 1.3 protocol)

**If Council Deadlocked**:
1. **Reframe the question** (maybe we're asking wrong thing)
2. **Bring in outside perspective** (new advisor, respected elder)
3. **Table and continue** (revisit after 2 weeks)
4. **Vote if stalled** (move forward with majority, review after experiment)
5. **Accept divergence** (if core values differ, maybe this isn't the right team)

### 6.3 Cultural/Values Conflicts

**If Team and Cultural Advisors Clash**:
- Values concerns are not overridden
- Team must engage deeply with the concern
- May require reframing or compromise
- Worst case: team member steps back if values irreconcilable

**If Users and Advisors Clash**:
- Both perspectives matter
- Advisors not gatekeepers, but protectors of sacred ground
- Seek solution that honors both concerns

## Feedback Integration

### 7.1 Collecting User Feedback

**Channels**:
- Monthly surveys (short, specific)
- Community forums (ongoing discussion)
- Direct feedback to community stewards
- Anonymous suggestion box
- Quarterly user council meetings (for representatives)

**What We Ask**:
- "What's working?"
- "What's frustrating?"
- "What would help you flourish more?"
- "Anything concerning or wrong?"
- "How is cultural respect showing up?"

### 7.2 Responding to Feedback

**Acknowledgment**: Every feedback gets acknowledged (even if can't act immediately)

**Categorization**:
- Bug reports → technical team
- Feature requests → prioritization process
- Values concerns → cultural advisors
- User experience issues → design review

**Follow-up**: "We heard you. Here's what we're doing about it." (even if answer is "not yet")

**Transparency**: Aggregated feedback published quarterly so community knows what we're hearing

## Scaling Principles

### 8.1 When Governance Needs to Change

**Trigger Points**:
- **50 users**: Add 2 community stewards to core team
- **100 users**: Formalize user representatives (election process)
- **200 users**: Create working groups for major areas
- **500 users**: Shift to representative council model
- **1000+ users**: Implement distributed governance with local circles

### 8.2 Transition Process

**No Abrupt Changes**:
1. **Propose change** to governance structure
2. **Discuss** why it's needed (what problems does it solve?)
3. **Trial period** (run both systems in parallel for 2-4 weeks)
4. **Vote** on whether to make permanent
5. **Document** transition with lessons learned

### 8.3 What Never Changes

- **No dictators**: No one person makes unilateral decisions
- **Transparency**: All major decisions explained publicly
- **Values priority**: Mission stays before convenience
- **User voice**: Users always have channel to affect decisions
- **Cultural respect**: Advisors always have veto on appropriation
- **Reversibility**: Major decisions can be unmade if they fail

## Three-Year Governance Roadmap

**Year 1 (Now)**: Core team with embedded cultural input
- 10-50 active users
- Quarterly strategic planning
- Monthly core team decisions
- Transparent decision logging

**Year 2**: Community council emerges
- 50-500 users
- Elected user representatives
- Working group structure forming
- Quarterly large-group decisions
- Distributed but coordinated

**Year 3+**: Federated governance
- 500+ users
- Local autonomy with core value alignment
- Working groups self-organized
- Annual strategic gathering
- Movement, not platform

## Living Governance

This is version 1.0. It will evolve as we learn what works.

**How We'll Know It's Working**:
- Decisions reflect user voice, not just leadership voice
- Conflicts resolved without hard feelings
- Cultural concerns caught and addressed early
- New governance structures form naturally (not imposed)
- Users feel ownership of KAHU
- Team stays small and mission-aligned
- No one burns out from decision-making overhead

**How We'll Course-Correct**:
- Quarterly governance review
- Annual community input on "is this working?"
- Willingness to change structure if it stops serving
- Documentation of what we learned
